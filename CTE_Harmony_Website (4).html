02 — Background

Not your grandfather's vocational class.

Career and Technical Education is a modern method of teaching that is integrated with academic learning and includes hands-on activities. It is a far cry from vocational training and guides students with a college-and-career-ready pathway. Results from the last several decades speak for themselves.

CTE programs are funded due to the Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act, reauthorized in 2018. The act stipulates that schools must fund and support CTE programs and that they serve all students equitably.

Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act — Perkins V
The federal law that funds and governs all CTE programs. It requires schools to serve every student equitably — including students with disabilities, English learners, low-income families, foster youth, and students preparing for nontraditional careers.

In addition to Clusters, students will also see Pathways — defined by multi-year course sequences known as Programs of Study. A student who takes an adequate number of courses becomes a CTE Concentrator. A student who completes the full sequence becomes a CTE Completer and often leaves with industry credentials in hand.

CTE fits Harmony perfectly.

Harmony School of Innovation already emphasizes Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. CTE in IT, Health Science, Engineering, and Business Management extends that identity into real credentials and career pathways.
02
Mission Alignment
Harmony's mission is college and career readiness. CTE delivers exactly that — dual enrollment, certifications, and work-based learning that makes a Harmony diploma even more powerful.
03
Equity Built In
Perkins V mandates equitable access for all students. That means CTE directly supports Harmony's commitment to serve every student regardless of background, ability, or income.
04
Flagship Potential
Schools that add CTE to a STEM-focused curriculum see increased enrollment and stronger community partnerships. Harmony Sugar Land could be a flagship CTE campus for the entire district.
"The introduction of CTE programs truly enhances the existing values of Harmony — it is the natural next step in fulfilling them."

What students actually gain.

CTE's advantages for high schoolers are clear, evident, and life-changing — not just empty promises, but results backed by research in thousands of high schools across the country.

08 — District Benefits

Beneficial for students. Strategic for Harmony.

Adding CTE at Harmony School of Innovation Sugar Land benefits students, and it is also a strategically sound decision for Harmony Public Schools as a district.

💰
Federal Funding
Perkins V funding goes directly to schools that set up CTE programs, covering equipment, teacher salaries, lab construction, and materials — at no cost to the district's general fund.
📈
Enrollment & Retention
Schools that implement effective CTE programs draw in and retain students through graduation. Families in Sugar Land are likely to select Harmony specifically because of CTE.
⭐
Stronger TEA Metrics
The Texas Education Agency measures graduation metrics, credentials earned, and post-secondary placements. CTE positively impacts all three and improves Harmony's state rating.
🤝
Industry Partnerships
Perkins V requirements include having local business professionals on advisory committees — creating direct partnerships with healthcare, technology, and local businesses.
🏆
District-Wide Model
Harmony School of Innovation Sugar Land has the opportunity to serve as the first flagship CTE campus for Harmony Public Schools — a model that could benefit the entire district.
🌟
Community Trust
A school that graduates students with actual credentials and work experience fosters a level of community trust that no advertisement can achieve.
"With thoughtful implementation, Harmony has the opportunity to become a regional leader in STEM-integrated CTE."
09 — Our Request

What we are respectfully asking.

We are respectfully requesting that Harmony Public Schools Central Office consider taking the following steps to begin exploring CTE implementation at Harmony School of Innovation Sugar Land. Each step is thoughtful and achievable, and together they offer a responsible path forward.

01
Initiate a CTE Feasibility Study
Determine which career clusters and pathways best align with our students, community workforce needs, and campus infrastructure.
02
Explore Carl D. Perkins Act Funding
Begin the formal application process for Perkins V funding — accessible to all public schools — which covers start-up costs, equipment, and CTE staffing at no burden to the district.
03
Develop a Phased CTE Strategy
Collaborate with campus leadership at Harmony School of Innovation Sugar Land to develop a thoughtful, phased CTE strategy with a clear timeline, budget, and accountability measures.
04
Recruit Certified CTE Educators
Recruit certified CTE teachers and industry experts who can bring real-world expertise and current industry standards into the Harmony classroom.
05
Form Industry Advisory Committees
Form local Sugar Land and Houston Region business, health, and technology advisory committees to ensure our programs stay aligned with real workforce requirements.
